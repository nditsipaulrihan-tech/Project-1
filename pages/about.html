Before I was an author, I was a daughter trying to understand her mother.

My name is Kum Doris Mbeuh. I am a Research Assistant and Data Analyst helping final year students at Degree, Masters and PhD level find clarity in their research and submit work they are proud of. I am the author of The Diary She Never Wrote a book I did not plan to write but could not stop writing. A deeply personal tribute to my mother. To her boots at 3am. To the envelope divided carefully on the kitchen table. To the dreams she never lived and the life she built for me from what remained. I am also the author of Clarity Before Hustle written for every ambitious person who is tired of working hard and going nowhere. Who wakes up motivated and goes to bed frustrated. Who has goals, has capacity, has dreams but lacks the one thing that makes all of it work.

Direction

I did not start with a writing course or a literary background.
I started with a real story. A clear voice. And an honest emotion.
It turns out that is all you ever need.

My Mission

To help people find clarity, build practical competence, and create meaningful opportunities for themselves and others.

What I Do

Research Assistance and Data Analysis

I help final year students at Degree, Masters and PhD level find clarity in their research, analyze their data, and defend their work with confidence.

Clarity Coaching

I work with ambitious professionals and entrepreneurs who are feeling scattered, stuck, or unclear about their next move.

Speaking and Workshops

I speak on clarity, personal development, making money online in Africa, research methodology, and building a personal brand that pays.

Author

I write books that speak to the African experience honestly, personally, and without apology.

A NOTE FROM DORIS

I used to think I had to have everything figured out before I could help anyone. Then I realized I am not teaching from the mountaintop. I am on the same path. A few steps ahead. Reaching back. That is who I am. Not the guru who has everything figured out.
But the woman who figured out enough to help you figure out yours.
